    MJLS:

    Consider the following homogeneous MJLS (\(\mathcal{G}\)):

    \[ \mathcal{G} = \begin{cases} x_{k+1} = A_{\theta_k}x_k + B_{\theta_k}u_k\\ z_k = C_{\theta_k}x_k + D_{\theta_k}u_k \end{cases} \]

    If \(\theta_k = i\):

    \[ A_{\theta_k} = A_i \in \{A_1, \ldots, A_N\} \quad B_{\theta_k} = B_i \in \{B_1, \ldots, B_N\} \\ C_{\theta_k} = C_i \in \{C_1, \ldots, C_N\} \quad D_{\theta_k} = D_i \in \{D_1, \ldots, D_N\} \]

    Quadratic Optimal Control Problem:

    The infinite horizon expected quadratic cost associated to \(\mathcal{G}\) is denoted by:

    \[ \mathfrak{J}(x_k,u_k,\theta_k) = \mathbb{E}\left[\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}{(x_k^*Q_{\theta_k}x_k + u_k^*R_{\theta_k}u_k)}\right] \]

    Quadratic Optimal Control Problem:

    And its correspondent in the finite horizon:

    \[ \mathfrak{J}(x_N,u_N,\theta_N,N) = \mathbb{E}\left[x_N*Q_Nx_N\sum_{k=0}^{N-1}{(x_k^*Q_{\theta_k}x_k + u_k^*R_{\theta_k}u_k)}\right] \]

    We want to design a controller that minimizes this cost.

    Operators:

    Assume that the Markov chain has time-invariant transition probabilities. Define the following operators:

    \[ \mathcal{E}(P) \triangleq (\mathcal{E}_1(P), \ldots, \mathcal{E}_N(P)) \] \[ \mathcal{E}_i(P) \triangleq \sum_{j=1}{N}{p_{ij}P_j} \]

    where \(P\) is the solution to a set of coupled Lyapunov equations. And \(p_{ij}\) is the component of the i-th row and j-th column of the transition probability matrix.

    Optimal Control - LQR:

    Mathematical programming (optimization) problem:

    \[ \bar{\mathfrak{J}}_k(x_k) = \mathbb{E}\left[\min_{u_k}x_k^*Q_{\theta_k}x_k + u_k^*R_{\theta_k}u_k + \mathfrak{J}_{k+1}(x_{k+1})\right] \]

    where the optimal cost at moment \(k\) is \(\mathfrak{J} = x_k^*P_kx_k\).

    Consider the Mode-Based Representation, when the Markov chain is in mode \(\theta_k = i\):

    \[ x_{k+1} = A_ix_k + B_iu_k \]

    Optimal Control - LQR:

    Making the substitution:

    \[ x_k^*P_ix_k = \min_{u_k} x_k^*Q_ix_k + u_k^*R_iu_k + x_{k+1}^*\mathbb{E}[P_i]x_{k+1} \] \[ x_k^*P_ix_k = \min_{u_k} x_k^*Q_ix_k + u_k^*R_iu_k + x_{k+1}^*\mathcal{E}_ix_{k+1} \]

    But we know that \(x_{k+1} = A_ix_k + B_iu_k\). Therefore:

    \[ x_k^*P_ix_k = \min_{u_k} x_k^*Q_ix_k + u_k^*R_iu_k + \\(A_ix_k + B_iu_k)^*\mathcal{E}_i(A_ix_k + B_iu_k) \]

    Optimal Control - LQR:

    Taking the derivative and setting it equal zero we get the optimal control input (\(\bar{u}\)):

    \[ \bar{u}_k = -(B_i^*\mathcal{E}_iB_i + R_i)^{-1}B_i^*\mathcal{E}_iA_ix_k \]

    Substituting it in the cost equation:

    \[ x_k^*P_ix_k = x_k^*\left(A_i^*\mathcal{E}_iA_i - A_i^*\mathcal{E}_iB_i(R_i + B_i^*\mathcal{E}_iB_i)^{-1}B_i^*\mathcal{E}_iA_i + Q_i\right)x_k \]

    Optimal Control - LQR:

    Therefore, the optimal matrices \(P_i \succeq 0\) are the unique solution of the Coupled Algebraic Riccati Equations (CAREs):

    \[ A_i^*\mathcal{E}_iA_i - A_i^*\mathcal{E}_iB_i(R_i + B_i^*\mathcal{E}_iB_i)^{-1}B_i^*\mathcal{E}_iA_i + Q_i \]

    Where:

    \[ R_i = D_i^*D_i \quad Q_i = C_i^*C_i \]

    Stabilization of MJLS via LMIs:

    Define:

    \[ \bar{u}_k = -K_ix_k \]

    Such that:

    \[ K_i = (B_i^*\mathcal{E}_iB_i + R_i)^{-1}B_i^*\mathcal{E}_iA_i \]

    Stabilization of MJLS via LMIs:

    We can rewrite the solution of the CAREs to grant stability via Lyapunov Theorem:

    \[ (A_i - B_iK_i)^*\mathcal{E}_i(A_i - B_iK_i) - P_i \prec 0, \quad \forall i \in \mathbb{K} \]

    Apply a congruence transformation with \(P_i^{-1}\):

    \[ P_i^{-1}(A_i - B_iK_i)^*\mathcal{E}_i(A_i - B_iK_i)P_i^{-1} - P_i^{-1}P_iP_i^{-1} \prec 0, \quad \forall i \in \mathbb{K} \]

    Stabilization of MJLS via LMIs:

    Let \(W_i = P_i^{-1}\) and \(Z_i = K_iW_i\), thus:

    \[ W_i(A_i - B_iK_i)^*\mathcal{E}_i(A_i - B_iK_i)W_i - W_i \prec 0 \] \[ (W_iA_i^* - Z_i^*B_i^*)\mathcal{E}_i(A_iW_i - B_iZ_i) - W_i \prec 0 \]

    Stabilization of MJLS via LMIs:

    Define:

    \[ \Phi_i = (A_iW_i - B_iZ_i) \]

    Therefore:

    \[ \Phi_i^*\left(\sum_{j=1}^{N}{p_{ij}W_j^{-1}}\right)\Phi_i - W_i \prec 0 \] \[ \sum_{j=1}^{N}{\sqrt{p_{ij}}\Phi_i^*W_j^{-1}\sqrt{p_{ij}}\Phi_i} - W_i \prec 0 \]

    Stabilization of MJLS via LMIs:

    And for any \(N\), applying the Schur complement yields:

    \[ W_i - \begin{bmatrix} \sqrt{p_{ij}}\Phi_i^* & \ldots & \sqrt{p_{ij}}\Phi_i^* \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} W_1^{-1} & \ldots & 0\\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots\\ 0 & \ldots & W_N^{-1} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \sqrt{p_{ij}}\Phi_i^*\\ \vdots \sqrt{p_{ij}}\Phi_i^* \end{bmatrix} \] \[ \begin{bmatrix} W_i & \star & \star\\ \sqrt{p_{ij}}(A_iW_i - B_iZ_i) & W_1 & \ldots & 0\\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots\\ \sqrt{p_{ij}}(A_iW_i - B_iZ_i) & 0 & \ldots & W_N \end{bmatrix} \succ 0 \quad \forall i \in \mathbb{K} \]
